# User Interviews

## Interview 1: PM

Role: System Engineer
Company Stage: MNC
Team Size: 10-100

What they told us:

"I usually use it to solve the problems I face while coding. ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini."
"I have a subscription to ChatGPT yearly package. Copilot is free for us."
"Output quality matters most when choosing an AI tool."
"I can use [the audit tool] if there are better alternatives available."

Most Surprising:
PM's assumption that Copilot is "free" was eye-opening. He doesn't track which tools are company-paid vs. personal subscriptions. This revealed that developers often don't have visibility into their actual AI spend across the team — they just use what's available. This is exactly the problem CredexIQ solves.
Design Impact:
This validated our core value prop: helping developers understand what they're actually spending on AI tools. Pranav's lack of awareness about subscription costs showed us we needed to:

Make the audit form simple enough for individual developers to fill out (not requiring IT access)
Show per-tool cost clarity in results
Highlight "free but actually paid by company" tools (like Copilot via GitHub Enterprise)

## Interview 2: SJ

Role: Computer Science Student
Company Stage: Student / Personal Projects
Team Size: Solo / Small college teams

What they told us:

"I mostly use ChatGPT and Gemini for assignments, debugging, and understanding concepts. Sometimes I try Claude when I need better coding explanations."

"Right now I only pay for ChatGPT Go. Mostly I use free versions because as a student I can’t afford multiple subscriptions."

"Yeah, sometimes ChatGPT Plus feels expensive for the amount I use monthly. I end up switching between free tools instead."

"For me, output quality matters first, then pricing. If the tool explains concepts clearly and helps me finish projects faster, I’ll use it."

"Yes, I’d definitely use something like [the audit tool]."

Most Surprising:

SJ's constantly rotates between free and paid AI tools depending on assignments, exams, and project deadlines. Instead of loyalty to one platform, students optimize for short-term value and flexibility. This showed us that AI spending confusion isn’t limited to companies — even students struggle to understand whether premium subscriptions are actually worth it.

Design Impact:

This interview helped us realize we should:

Include “best free alternative” recommendations in reports
Show value-for-money comparisons, not just pricing
Build simple explanations for non-technical users evaluating AI plans
Add student-focused recommendations and budget-friendly options

## Interview 4: VC

Role: Full Stack Developer
Company Stage: Mid-Size Tech Company
Team Size: 20-50 people

What they told us:

"Claude for coding, ChatGPT for solving errors and bugs."

"Claude is paid but honestly it gives good value for money because it helps a lot with coding."

"No, because if you want something simple, the freely available DeepSeek and ChatGPT versions already work well. Also, Claude isn’t that expensive."

"Quality matters the most."

"Yes, for analysis purposes I would use it. It’s actually a great idea."

Most Surprising:
VC didn’t view AI subscriptions as “expensive” if the output quality justified the cost. Instead of looking for the cheapest option, he evaluated tools based on productivity gains and coding efficiency. This highlighted that developers measure AI value differently than traditional software subscriptions.

Design Impact:
This interview reinforced the need to:

Focus on ROI and productivity, not just cheaper alternatives
Compare tools based on coding performance and use-cases
Include recommendations for free alternatives like DeepSeek when appropriate
Position CredexIQ as an optimization platform, not just a cost-cutting tool
